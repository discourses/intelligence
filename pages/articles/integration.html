The European Union Single Market

An investigation by Bertelsmann Stiftung

During $2014$ Bertelsmann Stiftung published an investigation addressing the question
... whether, and in what form, Germany and the other European core states have benefited from the continuous integration of Europe since the introduction of the European Union Single Market in $1992$.
The findings in relation to the United Kingdom are troubling. Foremost, it strengthens the argument that the country's state capacity and economic strength has been in trouble for decades, so much so ↠ it was barely able to take advantage of being part of an integrated market. Second, and a possible underlying cause of the previous point, the country has lost the wherewithal, and/or interest in, governing itself. The graph illustrates gains, or otherwise, per country.
